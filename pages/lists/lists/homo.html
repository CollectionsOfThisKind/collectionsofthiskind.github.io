Being human

Interesting items on human evolution and culture from "Ancestral DNA, Human Origins and Migrations"1:

Homo erectus did the heavy lifting: they had communal hunting; cared for the disabled; controlled fire; cooked; had toolmaking traditions; had shelters with stone foundations; probably used language; possibly used clothing and boats2.

The Flores population ("hobbits") may have been residual H. erectus or H. habilis, arriving c.1ma B.P. They likely lasted until 12ka B.P. overlapping with modern humans. Local folklore includes legends of small humanoids possibly indicating later survival3. Tall individuals with H. erectus features may have survived until 9ka in Australia4.

50k years ago, Neanderthals used deer ribs to smooth leather; we may have learnt it from them and the same tool is still used today5. They may also have passed on cave painting5.

We have been including grave goods in graves since at least 80–120ka B.P.6

The earliest human jewelry dates to at least 80–120ka B.P.6

Totem Poles probably pre-date the move to America and the earliest known is from 11ka B.P.; folk tales about the Raven may date from the same period.7.
