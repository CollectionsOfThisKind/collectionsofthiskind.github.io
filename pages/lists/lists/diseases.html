Old Diseases

A collection of antique English names for medical conditions:

  • Ague/s (fever; malaria)
  • Caligo (a speck on the eye causing low vision)
  • Cholic (intestinal cramping)
  • Chlorosis (an unknown disease of women, possibly anaemia, but more likely imaginary)
  • The Clap (gonorrhoea)
  • Consumption (tuberculosis)
  • The Courses (menstruation)
  • Dropsy (fluid swellings)
  • Festers (presumably sores of some kind)
  • Grippe (influenza)
  • King's Evil (scrofula, i.e. infection-caused swelling of the neck lymph nodes)
  • Lockjaw (tetanus)
  • The Mother (a unknown disease involving a feeling of rising strangulation said to be due to a wandering womb; possibly anxiety)
  • Palsy (a wide variety of paralyses, often with shaking)
  • Pin and Web (caligo and pterygium on the eye respectively, sometimes used together for cataracts)
  • The Pox (syphilis)
  • The Royal Disease (haemophilia)
  • Quinsy (abscess around the tonsils)
  • Saint Vitus' Dance (Sydenham's chorea; Huntington's)
  • The Stone (renal calculus)
  • Surfits (indigestion?)
  • The Sweats (unknown but deadly disease of the 15th and 16th C)
  • Tabes (syphilis, though more specifically the nerve pain tabes dorsalis)
  • Venery (nymphomania)

Bonus list of xenophobia:

  • The English Disease (Flagellation, Masochism)
  • The French Disease (Syphilis)
  • The German Disease (Homosexuality)
